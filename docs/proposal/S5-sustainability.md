# Section 5: Sustainability and future adoption

*AI4I Challenge 2026, Track 3 Development. Draft for review. Page budget: 1 page. Rubric anchor: C4 business model and feasibility (20).*

Svika was the overall winner of the GDG Harare Build with AI Hackathon 2026, a Google Developer Groups event themed on localisation, scoring 4.93 out of 5 from an independent judging panel.

## The business model: sell the record, not the ride

Svika does not tax the rider for safety. The main paying user is the owner and, through the owner, the network, because Svika produces something they cannot get today: a trustworthy record of what each kombi earns and carries. A light, optional rider tier sits beside that.

**Owner subscription.** Kombi owners pay a monthly presumptive tax set by ZIMRA Public Notice 51 of 2025, gazetted on 5 September 2025 and collected through the ZINARA licence: USD 50 a month for an 8 to 14 seater and USD 60 for a 15 to 24 seater, the kombi range, as a fixed amount rather than a share of takings. Since that notice no licence renews without a tax clearance, so the earnings record an owner brings to ZIMRA now decides whether the vehicle stays on the road, and today that owner has no real evidence of what the vehicle earned. Svika's dashboard, printable statement and revenue watchdog close that gap: a digital earnings record beside the fixed tax, with an isolation forest watching for leakage the eye cannot catch. That is the primary revenue line, a per vehicle subscription priced well under the leakage it surfaces and the credibility it buys with the tax authority.

**Conductor commission and the wallet float.** Conductors earn a commission on digital fares, carried in the ledger as a per fare rate (`commission_rate_bps`), which gives the crew a direct reason to clear fares digitally rather than route around the system. Unreturned change becomes USD wallet credit through the same double entry ledger; the aggregate float is a second, smaller economic base and the hook that keeps riders in the digital record.

**Rider convenience tier.** Riders never pay for safety. The free tier always carries trip planning, the boarding code, cash payment and the accessibility voice channel; paywalling any of those would break the product's own rules, cash always accepted and no paying rider ever buying a place in the queue ahead of a cash rider. A second tier, priced low, sells convenience only: saved routines, smart commute alerts, in ride voice guidance and offline route packs. It is a second revenue line beside the owner subscription, a model to price and test, not a fee we charge today.

## Cost structure

The cost side is deliberately light.

- **Compute.** The three models are CPU only, no GPU and no large weights (Section 2); server side inference on a modest allocation covers the network, and ZCHPC national compute is the intended long term home.
- **Data layer.** Managed Postgres on an entry tier today, with an in country move on the roadmap; the load is transactional and small per vehicle.
- **Display.** Map tiles are a licensed MapTiler service used only for display, a fixed cost that does not scale with rider count.
- **No per ride vendor cost.** Voice audio is pre generated and cached and every external provider sits behind an adapter, so no metered vendor call sits in the ride path to erode the margin. The unit economics are simple: revenue is per vehicle per month and the marginal cost of one more vehicle is close to zero, so the model survives on a few hundred kombis and improves with scale.

## Edge posture and resilience

Svika is server first by product rule, which answers the edge question head on. Inference runs on CPU on the ZCHPC national compute, never on a rider or conductor phone, so rider data stays off the device and in country, the sovereignty position Pillar 2 of the National AI Strategy asks for. The real edge risk, losing signal in traffic, is met by the offline first conductor app that clears fares from a local ticket cache and reconciles on sync.

## Licensing register

Svika keeps the licensing registry the terms of reference ask for. The field GPS traces, stop names and measured fares, and the synthetic histories generated by committed code, all sit under the repository licence. No third party dataset is used and none holds a real rider's personal data. The planned Shona training corpus, a paid human Golden Seed multiplied by filtered synthetic generation and evaluated on the FLORES-200 held out set, is roadmap: future data, not yet collected, and labelled as such in the dataset statement. Svika will hold the Tier 1 data controller licence under SI 155 before live processing (Section 4), and the register updates in the same commit as any change to a dependency or data source.

## Future adoption

Three roadmap lines extend the base without changing the promise. First, a Kombi Trust Value: a vehicle's clean record of trips and fares becomes a portable trust signal an owner can show a lender, an insurer or an association. Second, the voice guidance grows from a safety and accessibility feature into a Shona language tool the network can license, in line with the National AI Strategy's call for natural language processing in local languages. Third, the data itself is the moat: the same field logger any rider or conductor runs turns everyday travel into a growing record of real routes, stops and fares that no competitor and no city authority holds today. It runs on the Mount Pleasant Heights to Rezende corridor now; the wider network is the scaling plan, not a claim of present reach. That record stays consented, anonymised and aggregate only, never a rider's personal details, which a municipality or planning agency can use for the city transport planning Harare's master plan calls for, on the Nairobi precedent in Section 1. Each line here is labelled roadmap, earned only after the core product is trusted and paid for.
